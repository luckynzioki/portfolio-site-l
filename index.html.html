Measurement theory background

Classical Test Theory (CTT)
Foundation of psychometric analysis · BSc & MSc statistical methods
CTT models an observed score as the sum of a true score and measurement error: X = T + E. The framework underpins reliability estimation (Cronbach's α, split-half, test-retest) and item analysis through difficulty indices and point-biserial correlations.
Easy items (p > 0.70)
Moderate items (0.30–0.70)
Hard items (p < 0.30)
Item difficulty (p-value) and item-total correlation (point-biserial r) are the two core CTT item statistics.
Item Response Theory (IRT)
Modern psychometrics · 2PL and 3PL models
IRT models the probability of a correct response as a function of person ability (θ) and item parameters. The 3-parameter logistic model (3PL) incorporates discrimination (a), difficulty (b), and guessing (c).
High discrimination (a=1.8)
Moderate discrimination (a=1.0)
Low discrimination (a=0.5)
Item Characteristic Curves (ICCs) show the probability of a correct response across the ability continuum.
